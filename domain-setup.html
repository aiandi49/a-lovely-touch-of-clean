What We're Doing

Your new website is built and live — it's just sitting at a temporary web address right now. The last step is making your real domain name point to it, so when somebody types your business name into their browser, the new site is what comes up.

The easiest way to think about it
It's a mail forwarding order.

Your address stays your address. You still own it, you still pay for it, your name is still on it. We're just telling the post office "deliver to the new house from now on."

Same address. New destination.

What Doesn't Change

✓ Important — you're not giving anything up

Your domain stays 100% yours.

This is not a transfer. You are not signing it over to me or to anyone else.

  • You still own the domain. Your name, your account, your property.
  • It stays with ZenBusiness. You keep paying them for it exactly as you do now — same bill, same renewal.
  • You can undo this any time. Nothing here is permanent or locked. If you ever wanted to point it somewhere else, you could.
  • I don't get access to your ZenBusiness account. Unless you want me to have it — see the options below.

I'm mentioning all this up front because "let's move your domain" sounds a lot bigger and scarier than what we're actually doing, which is changing two settings.

How We Do It

Four steps. Most of the work is on my end. Here's the whole thing so nothing is a surprise.

1
I get the two settings we need
I do this · before we talk

I tell the hosting service that your domain is coming, and it gives me back two short lines of text. That's all this step is — I'm collecting two values to type in later.

2
We open your ZenBusiness account
Needs you · your login

This is the only part I can't do without you, because it's your account. You've got three options and any of them work fine:

  1. You log in and I talk you through it. You click, I tell you exactly what to type. Nothing gets typed you don't see.
  2. You give me temporary access and I do it in a few minutes. You can change your password afterward if you like.
  3. Neither of us touches it. ZenBusiness has a team that will enter the settings for you — we send them the two lines and they do it. This is an official thing they offer, and it's honestly the easiest option if you'd rather not fuss with it.

Whatever you're most comfortable with is the right answer. There's no wrong choice here.

3
We swap out the old settings
Together · about 10 minutes

Inside your account there's a section for your domain settings. There are some entries in there now pointing at ZenBusiness's own page. We take those out and put the new ones in.

Before I change a single thing, I take a screenshot of exactly how it looks. If anything goes sideways, that picture is how we put it back precisely the way it was. Nothing gets changed that can't be undone.

4
We wait, then check it
I watch this part

Once saved, the change spreads across the internet. Usually the site is live within minutes. Officially it can take up to a day or two for everyone everywhere to catch up.

The security padlock in the browser bar sets itself up automatically — you don't have to buy anything or do anything for that. I'll confirm it's working and let you know when it's fully done.

Your Email — Let's Be Careful Here

⚠ One question I need to ask you

Do you actually use service@lovelytouchcleaning.com?

Meaning — is that a real inbox you check, or does it forward somewhere else, or is it just printed on the site?

I ask because your email and your website both run off the same domain name, through different settings. They don't normally interfere with each other. But some systems will quietly rewrite the email settings when the website settings change — and if that happened, your email would stop working without any warning message.

So here's how I handle it, whether or not you think you use it:

  • Screenshot the email settings before we touch anything
  • Check them again after we save — same as before? Good.
  • Send a test message to that address and confirm it arrives

Takes an extra two minutes and removes the one real risk in this whole process. If anything did get changed, we'd catch it that same day instead of you wondering next week why nobody's emailing you.

Common Worries — Answered

"Will my website go down while we do this?"

Your current site keeps working right up until the new one takes over. There's no window where your address goes dead.

"What if I don't like the new site later?"

We point the domain back. It's the same two settings in reverse — nothing about this is one-way.

"Do I have to understand any of this?"

No. That's what this page is for. You need to know two things: your domain stays yours, and your email needs checking afterward. Everything else is my job.

"What if something breaks?"

I screenshot everything before I touch it, so we can always put it back exactly how it was. And ZenBusiness's support team can fix anything on their end if it comes to that.

"Does this cost anything?"

No. This is part of getting your site live — it's not part of the monthly booking system, and it's not a separate charge. You just keep paying ZenBusiness for the domain like you already do.
